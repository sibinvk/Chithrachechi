Krishnan Nair Shantakumari Chithra, known professionally as K.S. Chithra or simply Chitra, is an Indian playback singer and Carnatic musician. Fondly known as "Chinna Kuyil" (Little Nightingale) of South India, she is celebrated for her melodious voice and incredible versatility across languages and genres.

ğŸ“… Early Life

Born on July 27, 1963, in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, India, Chithra showed musical talent from an early age. She received formal training in Carnatic music, which became the foundation of her illustrious playback singing career.

ğŸ¤ Career Beginnings

Chithra made her playback singing debut in 1979 in a Malayalam film. Her breakthrough came in the 1980s when she started working with legendary composers like Ilaiyaraaja, M.S. Viswanathan, and K.V. Mahadevan.

The 1980s and 1990s marked her golden era. She became the voice of choice for leading actresses across South Indian cinema. Her collaboration with Ilaiyaraaja produced numerous hits, and she won her first National Film Award in 1986 for the Tamil film "Sindhu Bhairavi."
